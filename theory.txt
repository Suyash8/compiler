1. What is left recursion? Why must it be eliminated for LL(1) parsing?

Left recursion is a property of a grammar in which a non-terminal can derive a string that begins with itself. In other words, a grammar is left recursive if a non-terminal appears as the leftmost symbol in its own derivation.

There are two types of left recursion:

a) Direct left recursion:
A non-terminal directly calls itself.

Example:
A → Aα | β

Here, A appears immediately on the left side of the production Aα, making it directly left recursive.

b) Indirect left recursion:
A non-terminal derives another non-terminal that eventually derives back to the original non-terminal.

Example:
A → Bα
B → Aβ | γ

Here, A indirectly derives itself through B.

Left recursion must be eliminated for LL(1) parsing because LL(1) parsers are top-down parsers that expand productions from left to right using one symbol of lookahead. If left recursion exists, the parser enters infinite recursion.

For example:
A → Aα | β

When parsing A, the parser may repeatedly choose A → Aα, leading to:

A ⇒ Aα
⇒ Aαα
⇒ Aααα
...

This process never terminates and prevents successful parsing. Therefore, left recursion must be removed before constructing an LL(1) parser.

2. What is left recursion? Explain with an example.

Left recursion occurs when a non-terminal in a grammar derives itself as the leftmost symbol during derivation.

A grammar is left recursive if:

A ⇒+ Aα

where A is a non-terminal and α is any sequence of grammar symbols.

Example:

Consider the grammar:

E → E + T | T

This grammar is left recursive because E appears as the first symbol on the right-hand side of the production E → E + T.

Suppose we try to derive an expression:

E
⇒ E + T
⇒ E + T + T
⇒ E + T + T + T
...

The non-terminal E keeps expanding into itself without reaching a terminal symbol immediately. This is an example of direct left recursion.

To eliminate left recursion, the grammar can be rewritten as:

E → T E'
E' → + T E' | ε

Now the recursion is removed, and the grammar becomes suitable for LL(1) parsing.
